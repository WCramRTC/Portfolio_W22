About This Portfolio

1/8/2022

This is the start of the portfolio I'm making along with my students for the CSI-140 class. It's a kind of experiment. I rarely see teachers working alongside their students so I want to try something different. And I have a few idea's in mind for project pieces and feel this will light a fire under my bum to get them going and into the world.

I'm limiting myself only to the what students have been taught at the time. They are encouraged to go all out and ahead, but I feel this will be a good showcase example of whats expected for the assignments, and maybe a little encouragement, inspiration, or something to laugh at depending how bad it is.

Project - Game_W22

I've enjoyed playing idle games for the past few years. Something soothing yet anxiety building about something that requires both no and so much at the same time. Idle games, like Cookie Clicker, Idle Heros, and Idle Champions of the Forgotten Realm. I thought it might be a good time to try to build one of my own.

I plan to do this using only HTML, CSS, and JavaScript also. No frame works, and doing nothing that couldn't easily be googled by my students so they can do some of the fancier things. Like cookies for persistent information. This should give me a chance to refresh and update my skills while also creating something novel.

Update: 1/9/22

Long night, but a basic foundation was setup. I have a general layout down. There were a few key things I made sure to read up and implement.

CSS Grid Layouts: We've come a long way from messing with floats. I dove into Grid-Containers and Grid-Items. Implementing nested grids seems like it's going to be a very straight forward way of designing my gui. Whatever that ends up being.

Aync Timer in JavaScript: I had a feeling that I was going to need to find a way to control the refresh rate of a loop, and that ends up being "requestAnimationFrame()". A newer version of the setInterval(), this gets executed prior to the screen being refreshed, so that guarentees everything stays insync. It's not necessary for this project, but it's a great upgrade. It was late so I tracked down someones approach to doing exactly what I need, running a loop at 10fps. Brilliant and succint, I posted the link down below.
